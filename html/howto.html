how to

We tell you everything you need to make your trip to Norway as comfortable and easy as possible. From how to get to where to eat. In our tourist guide you will learn everything you need to be able to tour our country without having to worry about anything else. We provide you with the method to get your travel tickets through our search interface, as well as offer you all the necessary advice for your trip.

We are one of the most touristic countries in the world and we have everything prepared so that you feel at home. It doesn't matter if you travel alone or accompanied, with family or friends; We teach you how to visit Norway in the easiest way. Do not forget that our entire team is at your complete disposal to help you with everything you need. Do not hesitate to come to us for any problems and questions you have.

There are direct flights to Norway from many parts of the world and if you live in a neighboring country you can get there by boat, train or bus and even driving.

Oslo's is the country's main airport and where most international flights arrive. It has connections with more than 100 destinations. Keep in mind, however, that you may have to change planes before reaching your final destination.

Other international airports are located in and around Bergen, Kristiansand, Sandefjord, Stavanger, Tromsø and Trondheim.

There are direct flights to Norway from both coasts of the United States and from several cities in Europe. If you are traveling from Australia or New Zealand you have to stop at an airport in Asia, the Middle East or Europe.

All large cities and populations of a certain size have airports, both for domestic and international flights. There are more than 50 airports in Norway that make it possible to easily reach the most remote destinations, such as the Lofoten Islands, North Cape or Svalbard. Widerøe makes it easy to explore different parts of Norway. The airline flies to 41 destinations in Norway.

From Oslo airport you can reach the center of the capital in just 20 minutes thanks to the Flytoget express train. You can also catch various local buses and trains.

You can get to Norway from Sweden, Denmark and Finland.

There are buses leaving almost every hour from Gothenburg in Sweden and Copenhagen in Denmark. The bus service from Stockholm is also much more frequent than the trains. Several companies offer cheap tickets to travel between the largest cities in Norway, Denmark and Sweden. Generally, public transport in Norway is excellent. Buses, trains and ferries tend to run (mostly) on time, and cover most of the country.

Express buses connect major airports to nearby cities or ferry terminals, while long-distance coaches between cities often have more luxurious seats, and more space for luggage. The railroad also connects to bus lines, with every end station connected to bus routes to more remote towns.

If you’re traveling off the beaten track, the good news is that Norway’s public buses generally travel to even more remote areas of Norway. However, many buses on remote routes only run once or twice a day.

You’ll need to be organized, and plan your route carefully in advance if you want to take the bus in more remote areas.

If you arrive by car, you will most likely travel from Sweden, although Norway also has a land border with Russia and Finland. In any case, regardless of the country you arrive from, you will find customs controls at the border. In some points it is also necessary to pass a passport control.

The main roads leading to Norway are the E6 European road, which runs through Malmö, Helsingborg and Gothenburg in Sweden, before entering Norway at Svinesund in the south-east of the country, and the E8 road, which runs through Turku, Vaasa and Oulu. , in Finland, before moving on to Norway at Kilpisjärvi.

There are several car ferries between Norway (in Oslo and in the south of the country) and Denmark, Sweden and the Netherlands.

For a short stay, taking a car is pointless, but if like me you've got an extended trip, or if you're moving to Norway for a longer period, then taking a vehicle has many advantages.

Firstly, the minute you set off, your holiday commences. You can take detours, stop in cities you have never visited before and travel at your own pace.

The other plus point is that you can carry far more items with you when compared to flying; bikes, camping gear, sporting equipment etc. and it's much less hassle packing. Finally, if you plan on exploring Norway, a car makes things far more accessible for seeing Scandinavian scenery and landscapes.

There are several ferry routes from Denmark, Germany, Sweden and the Netherlands. In Denmark, ferries depart from Copenhagen, Frederikshavn, and Hirtshals. In Germany they leave from Kiel, in Sweden they leave from Strömstad and in the Netherlands from Eemshaven.

Most of the agencies offer service packages for cars that transport passengers and also discounts for retirees, students and children.

If you want to get to Norway with your own boat, there are marinas with facilities available all along the coastline.

Many cruise routes stop at Norwegian ports, especially in the fjord area and in coastal cities such as Bergen, Stavanger, Ålesund, Trondheim and Oslo. Some offer cruises to the Svalbard islands, Honningsvåg, Tromsø, Hammerfest and Bodø in northern Norway.

From Denmark, there are quite a lot of ferries to Norway. Most boats go from the north of Denmark. Here you will find the shortest crossings, but also the boat that sails to Stavanger and Bergen. This last place is the northernmost destination in Norway from Denmark.

There is also a crossing from the capital Copenhagen to Oslo. This is the longest crossing from Denmark to Norway.

The ferries from Denmark are especially interesting for those who prefer driving over a long ferry crossing. On the Fjordline Superfast ferry, you only have to spend around 2 hours and 15 minutes.

Norway has an extensive rail network that connects it with the rest of Scandinavia and Europe. There are frequent train connections to Oslo from Copenhagen, Stockholm and Gothenburg.

Most train journeys from mainland Europe occur overnight, and you'll find compartments with beds on all of them. There are several discount tickets available for train travel in Europe and Norway.

Once in Norway, it is very easy to reach both Fjord Norway and the rest of the country by train, including Northern Norway, on some of the most spectacular rail routes in the world.

Since railway in Norway is lengthy, there are many rail stations for the convenience of travelers. However, one of the more popular starting points for a Norwegian railway adventure is in the capital city Oslo.

Many of the Norway routes start with a train leaving the country's largest station - Oslo Central Railway Station. Thus, take a virtual stroll around the station by watching the video on the left and learn how you could be spending your time before departure.

restaurants

Get ready to sink your teeth into a feast of flavors in Norway. Enjoy a wide variety of delicious dishes made with both local and international products. Let yourself be guided by the fresh aroma of our local products and find a free table. Maybe even one with a view. In Norway you will undoubtedly be able to live a culinary experience according to your palate (and your budget).

More and more passion for gastronomy is awakening and this is generating a new wave of openings throughout the country, from the largest cities to the most remote places.
