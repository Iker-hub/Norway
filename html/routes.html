routes

Along selected roads in Norway, natural wonders are amplified by art, design, and architecture designed to bring you closer to nature in new and surprising ways. From the far north to the deep southwest, Norwegian architecture and design have stepped into the landscape to intervene in the scenery alogn particularly beautiful stretches of road. You'll find unexpected rest stops, breathtaking viewpoints and astonishing art installations. Meet the award-winning Scenic Routes.

Norwegian Scenic Routes are selected roads that run through landscapes with unique natural qualities, along coasts and fjords, mountains and waterfalls. The routes are intended as alternatives to the main roads, and the drive itself should be an enjoyable experience.

  1. The Alaska boundary dispute, simmering since the Alaska Purchase of 1867, became critical when gold was discovered in the Yukon during the late 1890s, with the U.S. controlling all the possible ports of entry. Canada argued its boundary included the port of Skagway.

    The dispute went to arbitration in 1903, but the British delegate sided with the Americans, angering Canadians who felt the British had betrayed Canadian interests to curry favour with the U.S.

  2. Convinced that Canada had proven itself on the battlefields of Europe, Prime Minister Robert Borden demanded that it have a separate seat at the Paris Peace Conference in 1919. This was initially opposed not only by Britain but also by the United States, which saw such a delegation as an extra British vote.

    Borden responded by pointing out that since Canada had lost nearly 60,000 men, a far larger proportion of its men, its right to equal status as a nation had been consecrated on the battlefield.

Bud, between the
islets and reefs

Bud is a visual delight as it curves elegantly from islet to islet over its seven bridges. Nature and modern engineering meet and create this highly unique driving experience.

The trip takes you from the fertite cultural landscape of the coast across moorland to bare crags along the weather-beaten, open bay of Hustadvika. Take time to watch the sun disappearing below the horizon from here. Atlanterhavsvegen has been described as the world's most beautiful drive.

activities

winter fun

cross-country ski

In Norway you can do cross-country skiing almost anywhere in the country. Some destinations are, however, especially suitable and fun for ski fans, whether they are great athletes, families or beginners.

Here you can experience the best of the Norwegian winter, with and without skis. Sign up for a guided excursion or join a ski course. The next day you can always recover your strength on a sled, whether sliding down a slope or letting yourself be carried by a group of dogs. Norway's main alpine ski resorts also offer fantastic opportunities for cross-country skiing. They have an excellent network of slopes where you can easily combine various activities.

art and culture

the new munch museum

In the center of Oslo, a new building stands next to the fjord that has transformed the architectural skyline of the city. Apart from the exhibitions, the museum hosts different programs of shows, literature, music, cinema and dance.

The objective that has been pursued with the construction of the new MUNCH is to make the city more attractive to Oslense, as well as providing another incentive to visit the Norwegian capital.

The Spanish architecture studio Estudio Herreros is behind the design of this extremely tall sixty-metre building, which has managed to reduce the emissions of other buildings.

world heritage sites

From ancient petroglyphs to impressive fjordside landscapes to early industrialization sites: Norway has nine places and areas that, due to their cultural and natural value, are part of the UNESCO World Heritage List.

One of the missions of Unesco is the conservation and protection of natural landscapes and irreplaceable historical buildings and monuments.

The choice of these places is based on ten criteria that include innovation, historical relevance or unique nature. These World Heritage Sites are spread all over the world, today there are more than 1,000 and nine of them are in Norway.
