routes

Along selected roads in Norway, natural wonders are amplified by art, design, and architecture designed to bring you closer to nature in new and surprising ways. From the far north to the deep southwest, Norwegian architecture and design have stepped into the landscape to intervene in the scenery alogn particularly beautiful stretches of road. You'll find unexpected rest stops, breathtaking viewpoints and astonishing art installations. Meet the award-winning Scenic Routes.

Norwegian Scenic Routes are selected roads that run through landscapes with unique natural qualities, along coasts and fjords, mountains and waterfalls. The routes are intended as alternatives to the main roads, and the drive itself should be an enjoyable experience.

the road to the artic ocean

varanger

A drive in the fluctuating light of the ocean and sky-towards the expanse of ocean and unique rock landscape

  • From: Varangerbotn
  • To: Hamningberg
  • Length: 160 km
  • Altitude: 0 - 123 masl

  1. Day 1

    Varangerbotn
    arctic road

    Varangerbotn is about the long lines in the landscape, as well as the long lines of history. Proximity to Russia, Finnish immigration and Sami traditions provide the area with exciting cultural diversity.

    The drive runs through birch woods, marshland and a rugged cliff landscape. The light is special and the bird life and wildlife unique. Species of birds that you won’t find anywhere else in the world are relatively easily accessible. There is also a good chance of experiencing reindeer at first hand along this road.

  2. Day 2

    Nesseby's
    white church

    From Nesseby village it is only a short walk down to the fjord and the landmark of Nesseby church.

    The scorched-earth tactics used by the retreating German army during the Second World War left Finnmark and northern Troms counties in flames. Miraculously Nesseby church remained unscathed. The church was built in 1858 and has a baroque altarpiece dating from around 1720.

  3. Day 3

    Domen's
    flok monument

    Domen is known from folklore as the place where witches would have their meetings with Satan. At the top of this mountain, has been established a shelter for resting and enjoying the majestic view.

    The project consists of three small buildings positioned to provide shelter and protect you from the elements. The buildings have steel frameworks, with whitewashed pine panelling on the outside and Norwegian Kebony wood on the inside. The windows are lightly tinted in pink to prevent birds from flying into the glass.

  4. Day 4

    Steilneset's
    architecture

    Steilneset memorial in Vardø has been erected to commemorate the 91 people who were found guilty of witchcraft and burnt at the stake here.

    The artist Louise Bourgeois and the architect Peter Zumthor have joined in creating evocative space, a burning chair and a 100-metre long memorial hall that communicate this gripping story.

    The texts are based on the old court records and are written by Liv Helene Willumsen, Professor of History.

  5. Day 5

    Hamningberg
    Bird-watching hide

    Along Varangerfjorden we have developed a series of bird hides in places where birds gather and stay.

    Varanger is one of the few places in the world where you can easily seek out Arctic birds in their natural habitat. Such birds are usually only found in inaccessible places such as Siberia and northern Canada.

    From the hide in Hamningberg you can study these birds and enjoy the Arctic scenery, well protected from wind and weather.

along the blue mountains

rondane

A drive that offers close encounter with Norwegian nature in the easily accesible borderland between mountains and landscapes

  • From: Enden
  • To: Folldal
  • Length: 75 km
  • Altitude: 700 - 1060 masl
  1. Day 1
  2. Day 2
  3. Day 3
  4. Day 4
  5. Day 5
  6. Day 6

  1. Day 1

    Enden encounter
    with nature

    Like ancient slumbering giants, the mountains line the road that takes you alongside Rondane National Park. The slowly changing landscape with its towering mountains is a memorable experience.

    2,000 metre-high summits in a singular landscape decked in blinding on the glittering snow, the bluish light. The quiet tranquility. Perhaps it is this feeling that has made so many artists and writers find inspiration in Rondane - and maybe this is why people return to Rondane again and again over generations.

  2. Day 2

    Sollia's
    wooden church

    In 1720 a law was introduced that required people to go to church twice each year.

    The inhabitants of the Setningsdalen valley had to make the long trek across the mountains to Ringebu, and on one occasion an entire group of travellers froze to death. In 1732, the king granted permission to build a new church, but the inhabitants of Sollia did not receive any funds for construction. As a result, the local parishioners mustered the ground, timber and stone to build the church themselves. The timbered church was completed in 1738.

  3. Day 3

    Atnbrufossen
    museum

    The Atnbru area is one of the most outstanding cultural landscapes in Hedmark county.

    Fossehuset, the museum at Atnbrufossen waterfall, lies in a verdant birch grove. The Water Works Museum represents the old building custom in timber, while the new Fossehuset shows the modern use of wood as a building material. The building was formerly a sawmill with timber-floating facilities and a hydropower station, but is now also a venue for art exhibitions and concerts.

  4. Day 4

    Strømbu gateway

    Strømbu is one of the main gateways to the Rondane massif and an excellent starting point for hikes in what was Norway’s first national park. The rest area has a roof terrace and a lounge directly adjacent to the river Atna that winds between pine trees and sand banks. The roof terrace provides a place to rest and admire the view of the mountains and the river.

    The very first ideas of the project were quite simple and maybe somewhat banal: to make the building as a first movement - a step or a small steep hill towards the distant mountains.

  5. Day 5

    The impressive
    mines of Folldal

    The Folldal Mines are one of Norway’s foremost technical and industrial heritage sites, with approximately 70 well preserved buildings.

    The mines were in operation from 1748 to 1993, producing copper, zinc and sulphur. Barracks for the workers and the director’s mansion containing the luxuries of the era bear witness to the class distinctions that characterised this mining community. Below the mining installations there is a car park with an information board and a viewing point.

the road to across the sea

atlanterhavsvegen

"The road across the sea" is the iconic experience with bridges that arch between the islets and reefs along the rough Hustadvika Bay

  • From: Bud
  • To: Kårvåg
  • Length: 36 km
  • Altitude: 0 - 30 masl
  1. Day 1
  2. Day 2
  3. Day 3
  4. Day 4
  5. Day 5
  6. Day 6

  1. Day 1

    Bud, between the
    islets and reefs

    Bud is a visual delight as it curves elegantly from islet to islet over its seven bridges. Nature and modern engineering meet and create this highly unique driving experience.

    The trip takes you from the fertite cultural landscape of the coast across moorland to bare crags along the weather-beaten, open bay of Hustadvika. Take time to watch the sun disappearing below the horizon from here. Atlanterhavsvegen has been described as the world's most beautiful drive.

  2. Day 2

    Kjeksa's recycling
    architecture

    The viewing point at Kjeksa lies near the fishing village of Bud on Atlanterhavsvegen. From the rest area a magnificent view opens to the shipping lane, the ocean and Hustadvika bay, one of Norway’s most infamous stretches of sea. Paths and steps lead down to the edge of the sea, to places where anglers can try their luck.

    Innovation in use of material with local significance has become a tool to create new public links between historical and natural values and the future tourist.

  3. Day 3

    Askevågen's
    marine life

    The viewing point at Askevågen provides opportunity for a close encounter with the Atlantic Ocean. The platform is located at the end of the breakwater, with glass walls for protection against the weather and spray. This little point provides a 360-degree view of the ocean, the archipelago and the mountainous shore.

    There are varied lodging options along the Atlantic Ocean Road, with some stunning locations available.

  4. Day 4

    Farstadsanden's
    rest area

    Farstadanden in Hustadvika is an easily accessible recreation area along Scenic Route Atlanterhavsvegen. Its rich bird and plant life and characteristic sand dunes give the area a uniqueness that is worth preserving. Farstadbukta bay area is therefore protected as a nature reserve.

    From the beach at Farstadsanden you can take a 2.5 km walk (one way) out to Nordneset Lighthouse. The coastal trail starts at the end of the beach and follows the coastline to the lighthouse. The walk is quite an experience, in sunshine as well as in stormy weather.

  5. Day 5

    Kårvåg, connection
    with nature

    Visitors may safely try their hand at angling on specially built fishing walkways on either side of the Kårvåg bridge. In the strong tidal current you can pull cod, pollock, mackerel and coalfish out of the clear, pure ocean water. One of these anglers’ bridges ends in a circular platform provided with benches. On the other side of the bridge, stairs lead down to the rock from where travellers can safely try their hand at fishing.

activities

winter fun

cross-country ski

In Norway you can do cross-country skiing almost anywhere in the country. Some destinations are, however, especially suitable and fun for ski fans, whether they are great athletes, families or beginners.

Here you can experience the best of the Norwegian winter, with and without skis. Sign up for a guided excursion or join a ski course. The next day you can always recover your strength on a sled, whether sliding down a slope or letting yourself be carried by a group of dogs. Norway's main alpine ski resorts also offer fantastic opportunities for cross-country skiing. They have an excellent network of slopes where you can easily combine various activities.

art and culture

the new munch museum

In the center of Oslo, a new building stands next to the fjord that has transformed the architectural skyline of the city. Apart from the exhibitions, the museum hosts different programs of shows, literature, music, cinema and dance.

The objective that has been pursued with the construction of the new MUNCH is to make the city more attractive to Oslense, as well as providing another incentive to visit the Norwegian capital.

The Spanish architecture studio Estudio Herreros is behind the design of this extremely tall sixty-metre building, which has managed to reduce the emissions of other buildings.

world heritage sites

From ancient petroglyphs to impressive fjordside landscapes to early industrialization sites: Norway has nine places and areas that, due to their cultural and natural value, are part of the UNESCO World Heritage List.

One of the missions of Unesco is the conservation and protection of natural landscapes and irreplaceable historical buildings and monuments.

The choice of these places is based on ten criteria that include innovation, historical relevance or unique nature. These World Heritage Sites are spread all over the world, today there are more than 1,000 and nine of them are in Norway.
